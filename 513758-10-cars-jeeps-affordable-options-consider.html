10 cars that look like Jeeps: Affordable options to consider

In the Car Hall of Fame, if there is one, the Jeep is the most deserving contender. The car's awe-inspiring design and reliability aren't contestable, especially when off-roading. Its robust frame and solid suspension have inspired numerous models that could easily pass as Jeeps. And that is expected when a well-made car sets the benchmark for rugged SUVs. So, what other cars that look like Jeeps do you know?

One thing about the Jeep is that its sturdy build is perfect for off-road performance. Unfortunately, you might be unable to drive it for numerous reasons.

The good thing is that you have other visually and functionally comparable brands in the market that can pass off as one. So, what cars like Jeep Wrangler do you know about?

What if you went for another cheap or expensive car that looks like a Jeep? You might be surprised that so many vehicles are similar to the Jeep in design and amenities. Which ones are the best in the market?

1. Nissan Xterra

The Nissan Xterra is your perfect response to "What cars look similar to a Jeep?” People in the market for an off-road automobile and don’t want to go for the Jeep usually settle for this excellent model. The Nissan Xterra is strikingly similar to the Jeep in appearance and functionality. However, the cars have a slight difference in terms of horsepower.

The Nissan Xterra has a 4.0L V6 engine that produces 261 horsepower. The Jeep has a 3.6L V6 engine and delivers 285 horsepower; therefore, it is stronger than the Nissan Xterra, but by a small margin.

2. Suzuki Jimmy

Among cars that look like jeeps but aren't is the Suzuki Jimmy. This brand is quite similar to the Jeep in numerous aspects. It is a descendant of the original Suzuki 4x4, the 1970s LJ10. Like the Jeep, Suzuki Jimmy is a perfect offroad automobile with a box body shape.

3. Ford Bronco

The Ford Bronco’s incredible design isn’t the only thing that attracts people; its impressive performance matches the Jeep and is something to remember the next time you are looking for a Jeep alternative.

The car is comfortable and driver-friendly on the road, and it even has a more significant advantage over Jeep for off-road enthusiasts. The Ford Bronco has a narrow body and a short wheelbase.

4. Toyota 4Runner

Although Toyota 4Runner isn't a new vehicle that looks like a jeep, it has been in the market long enough to gain a good following from car enthusiasts who have found it impressive.

The Toyota 4Runner is more significant than the Jeep. The Jeep Wrangler is 184.9 inches long, 73.7 inches wide, and 72.6 inches high, while the Toyota 4Runner is 191.3 inches long, 75.8 inches wide and 71.5 inches high. And this means you get extra space for your cargo when on a hiking expedition.

5. Hummer H2

The Hummer H2 is another fantastic automobile among the collection of cars like the Jeep Wrangler. Like the Jeep, the car quickly gained icon status after its release in 2002. The military-styled automobile isn’t only about being perfect for handling challenging terrain but has the sturdiness and comfort you would want for an extended cruise.

Although it dropped from the market in 2009, it is still a classic, and its electric counterpart is even more impressive. It is just as powerful as the original Hummer H2.

6. Mercedes Benz G-Class

The Mercedes Benz G-Class is your answer to the question, “Which car looks like Jeep Wrangler? You can easily confuse it for a Jeep. The car model looks strikingly similar to the Jeep in design and performance. Initially designed for German military use, the car is now a trusted road monster.

You would pay a little more for the Mercedes Benz G-Class than the Jeep, but you are assured that the car will be perfect wherever you need it.

7. Suzuki Samurai

Without knowledge of cars, or a novice, you can easily have this immaculately designed Suzuki Samurai in your collection of Jeep automobiles because of the striking similarity. A first look would have you dismiss it as among the types of Jeeps you don't know much about.

The Suzuki Samurai is a four-wheel drive, which means its engine sends sufficient power to the front and wheel axles. It also comes with a factory bulbar (not attractive) that provides the desired protection against damage in a collision.

8. Toyota FJ Cruiser

Undoubtedly, Toyota made the Toyota FJ Cruiser to compete with the Jeep Wrangler considering the striking similarity. The large car manufacturer targeted the massive off-road market with this model, which worked well considering its affordability compared to the Jeep.

Another great thing about the car is that it offers a manual transmission option that gives you more control.
