What is Underbite?

The lower teeth and jaw protrude further forward than the upper teeth when closed, causing an underbite. When a person closes their mouth, the lower jaw protrudes, putting lower teeth in front of upper teeth. Misalignment impacts tooth and jaw function and appearance. Underbites may need orthodontics, jaw surgery, or both, depending on severity.

Several reasons may cause an underbite, including:

  • Genetics
  • Oral habits, such as thumb sucking or tongue thrusting
  • Trauma to the face or jaw
  • Birth defects

Underbites can cause a number of problems, including:

  • Difficulty chewing
  • Speech problems
  • Jaw pain
  • Tooth damage
  • Self-consciousness

Underbites may be repaired with braces or Invisalign. Surgery may be needed in extreme situations.
