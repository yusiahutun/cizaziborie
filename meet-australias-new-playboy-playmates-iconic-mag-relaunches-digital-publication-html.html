Meet Australia's new Playboy Playmates as iconic mag relaunches as digital publication

Publish date: 2024-07-09

Playboy is entering the digital age with its own creator-led platform that promises to be an 'elevated' and porn-free alternative to the X-rated OnlyFans.

The site has already attracted a number of Australian stunners, from established influencers and reality stars to up-and-coming models.

Unlike OnlyFans, which allows almost anybody over 18 to join, Playboy creators must be approved by the platform's editorial team to ensure they meet the high standards of the heritage brand.

The biggest Australian name on the website so far is Gabby Epstein, who boasts three million Instagram followers and covered Playboy Mexico in 2021.

Some of the newcomers on the platform include beauty influencer Jen Suluka, blonde vixen Mia Smith, and glamour models Tayla Perrin and Carly Louise Lippiatt.

Reality star Megan Marx, best known for The Bachelor and MTV's The Challenge, has become one of the top creators on Playboy since joining the site earlier this month.

Just two weeks after being approved as a creator, Megan was named the 'Centerfold of the Day' on Playboy.com and became the top trending model on the website too.

Megan previously appeared topless on the cover of Maxim Australia with fellow Bachelor star Tiffany Scanlon, with whom she was in a relationship at the time.

The 32-year-old, who grew up in a strict religious cult, said it was Playboy's dedication to sexual freedom that inspired her to sign up.

Social media star Gina Stewart, 52, is one of the oldest models on Playboy.com and said she was impressed by the brand's 'iconic status' and classier image compared to more explicit platforms.

'The Playboy platform has launched, yes, and it's going to give OnlyFans a run for its money,' she told the Daily Star.

'It will stay within the Playboy guidelines, nudity but not pornography,' she added.

'I think this appeals to a more conservative demographic audience and is a lot more classy and glamorous.'

The Playboy app launched in 2021 under the name Centerfold, with rapper Cardi B attached as both a founding member and creative director.

The revamped magazine, which stopped printing in 2020 due to financial woes, will officially launch later this year.

Unlike OnlyFans, which permits creators to share hardcore pornography, Playboy is more focused on nudity and photography, much like the classic style of the magazine

The online format for the magazine allows users to access the brand's 'creator platform', which the company is striving to brand as an 'elevated, safe and exclusive' alternative to OnlyFans.

Instead of Playboy's signature 'bunnies', users interact with 'creators' who post uncensored content on their pages and give subscribers exclusive access into their lives, in addition to posing for the magazine's digital covers.

Amanda Cerny, a comedian and model who became a Playmate in 2011, is the outlet's first-ever digital cover model.

She made more than $1million as a creator during the brand's rollout of the beta version of the Playboy app, which was then called 'Centerfold'.

'From becoming the Playmate centerfold of October 2011, to now being in the company of Marilyn Monroe, Dolly Parton and Kate Moss on Playboy's cover, is a dream come true!' Cerny said in a statement.

'A Playboy creator is someone who is able to embrace their creativity, their sexuality and freely and unapologetically express themselves.

'I can't wait to share more of my journey with exclusive behind-the-scenes content with my fans on my Playboy channel.'

The company's Chief Brand Officer Rachel Webber told the New York Post: 'Our Playboy creator platform is the Playboy magazine for the 21st century.

'We're putting the power of content creation in the hands of the creative community and giving them the tools to interact with and monetize their fanbases directly.'

One major difference between OnlyFans and Playboy is that while anyone can sign up to become an OnlyFans creator, Playboy creators have to apply and be accepted by the brand's editorial team.

Like OnlyFans, users will be able to subscribe and pay to view adult content and interact with the women and men creating it. Some, who pay for it, will also be granted 'access' to the daily lives of the models.

The platform will allow nudity, which its creators view as a form of art and free expression, but explicit content and pornography will not be allowed.

Ben Kohn, the company's CEO said he hopes the interjection of Playboy's creators will disrupt the current creator ecosystem in much the same way that 'Playboy magazine shook up the publishing industry nearly 70 years ago.'

After printing its final cover in March 2020, the magazine laid off 25 staffers.

The new digital magazine, said the company, will include contributor essays from creators and content about what it's like to be a part of Playboy.

Many former reality stars who signed up for the platform have progressed from doing lingerie photos to hardcore porn on the site to keep their subscribers paying.
