Table of Biography

Cole Sprouse is an American actor and photographer. Cole Sprouse started as a child actor alongside his twin brother Dylan. Their most popular show is “Suite Life of Zack and Cody”. His solo projects include “Riverdale” and “Five Feet Apart” among others.

Early Life and Childhood

Cole Sprouse was born on 4 August 1992 and currently is 31 years old. His parents are his father Matthew Sprouse and his mother Melanie Wright. Similarly, his parents were teachers at an English language school in Tuscany, Italy. He has a twin brother Dylan Sprouse and together the twins are called the “Sprouse Brothers/Bros”. Moreover, he is 15 minutes younger than Dylan. They together won the hearts of many viewers after the twins starred in the Disney show The Suite Life of Zack & Cody.

Both the twin brothers have dual citizenship in Italy and the USA. Moreover, talking about his childhood, he began snowboarding at age four. Similarly, he was born in Arezzo, Italy, but doesn’t have Italian ancestry. Instead, he has English, Scottish, Danish, and German ancestry. Likewise, he was named after jazz singer and pianist Nat King Cole.

His parents moved from Italy to their native California only four months after the twins’ birth. Hence, they grew up in Long Beach. Then in 2012, the Sprouse family moved from Long Beach to Calabasas in California. Moreover, the twins’ grandmother’s name is Jonine Booth Wright and she was an actor and drama teacher during her younger days. She was also responsible for the pair’s appearance in TV commercials at the young age of 6 months.

He and Dylan are good friends with Kyle Massey of That’s So Raven. The Sprouse family also had three dogs. Bubba belonged to Dylan and he sadly passed away in 2017, Pinky is their father, and Curry belongs to their stepmother.

Cole Sprouse – Education

While filming The Suite Life of Zack & Cody, the Sprouse Brothers received on-set tutoring for three hours daily for school. The brothers were getting “straight A’s and honors”. Then, in their last year of high school, the Sprouse brothers took Advanced Placement classes where Cole chose AP Psychology and AP Government and Dylan chose AP Psychology and AP Spanish.

In the year 2010, the Sprouse brothers joined New York University. They initially planned to attend the university in the fall of 2010 but they deferred for one year. He and Dylan attended the Gallatin School of Individualized Study which allows students to plan their curriculum. He focused on humanities and archaeology whereas Dylan focused on video game design.

Later, he studied archaeology, geographic information systems, and satellite imaging at New York University. Dylan and Riverdale co-star Mendes were also fellow students at NYU. Both brothers graduated in May of 2015, walking away with the diploma together. As a prank, the twins switched places to get their diplomas. After graduation, he took a job as an archaeological assistant in an artifact laboratory in Brooklyn, where he was working when he read the script for Riverdale.

Cole Sprouse – Career and Professional Life

Cole Sprouse is an actor, entrepreneur, and photographer best known for his role as Cody Martin in The Suite Life of Zack & Cody and its spin-off The Suite Life on Deck.

As with many twins, he and his brother have often played the same role, due to child labor laws in California restricting the number of times children can be filmed in a day. Casting twins in a single role thus allows more time for the character to be filmed.

Similarly, he has been on screen from the young age of six months. The twins started appearing in TV commercials advertising diapers at such a young age. He also gained fame for his role in the comedy film Big Daddy (1999). For this, the twins play the single role of Julian McGrath. They also played the same role in the show Grace Under Fire as Patrick Kelly at a young age. The twins played a small role in a thriller titled The Astronaut’s Wife in the year 1999.

More details on Combined Projects

He has noted that his childhood entry into acting alongside his twin resulted from their family’s need for money. However, he came to appreciate many of the opportunities his early career presented him with.

The Sprouse brothers shared the role of Patrick, the youngest son of Brett Butler’s character on the TV show Grace Under Fire that broadcasted from 1993 to 98. Then in 1999, they made their feature film debut playing Adam Sandler’s son in Big Daddy. Moreover, they were seen as a set of alien twins in the movie The Astronaut’s Wife.

Then, the Sprouse brothers reached a much higher level of fame with their Disney Channel sitcom The Suite Life of Zack and Cody in which he played Cody who is the more conscientious of the on-screen twins. Suite Life is a top-rated series that ran from 2005 to 2008 on Disney Channel.

Similarly, the Sprouse brothers also headlined a Suite Life TV movie and the spinoff The Suite Life on Deck that broadcasted from 2008 to 2011. In addition, Suite Life characters, Zack and Cody, also showed up on other Disney hits like That’s So Raven, Wizards of Waverly Place, and Miley Cyrus’ Hannah Montana.

Solo Career

The projects that Cole undertook which Dylan is not a part of include Friends from 2000 to 2002. For this, he played the character, Ben Geller, for seven episodes. Ben is the son of David Schwimmer’s character Ross. Working on the set of Friends, it is believed that he developed a crush on Jennifer Aniston.

Similarly, he also played the role of Jughead Jones in Riverdale since 2017. Then he appeared in Five Feet Apart, as the character Will Newman in 2019. He credits social media for facilitating his stepping back into the spotlight. She also told People magazine in 2017:

“I anticipated we would go to college and then we would fade out, that was kind of what we wanted at the time, but social media hung onto my brother and I, and thankfully fans hung onto my brother and I, and I think it’s one of the reasons that this reemergence can come back with a little more strength, and I’m very thankful for that.”

Along with Dylan, he became a successful American child star and as an adult, he embraced photography and made the choice to return to acting with the television series Riverdale.

More Details on Solo Career

After taking time out to get a college degree, he started his adult acting career with the CW series Riverdale. It is a noirish spin on the world of Archie Comics and it premiered in January 2017. Though he’d initially been up for Archie, he was drawn to the character of Jughead Jones. The character Jughead is an outsider who serves as the show’s narrator.

Moreover, he was cast as Jughead but had to dye his blonde hair black for the role. Later he noted that this change makes it easier for people to distinguish him from his twin Dylan. Other stars in this successful series include KJ Apa as Archie, Camila Mendes as Veronica, and Lili Reinhart as Betty Cooper.

Further, he also appeared in Five Feet Apart. He played the character William “Will” Newman in this 2019 American romantic drama film. His co-star in the film was Haley Lu Richardson. The pair played cystic fibrosis patients who developed feelings for one another. Though the film was a commercial success, it drew mixed reviews, and some in the cystic fibrosis community criticized it for the trivialization of a series of illnesses.

Beyond Acting

Moreover, he is a talented photographer. He has also credited photography as offering a positive outlet when he was experiencing depression. He has photographed magazine covers and fashion shoots. His works have appeared in publications like The Sunday Times and L’Uomo Vogue. So far, he has photographed model Kendall Jenner, Game of Thrones actress Sophie Turner, singer Sam Smith, and his Riverdale co-star Lili Reinhart among many others.

Talking about his passion for photography with The Sunday Times in 2017, he said:

“I’ve been dabbling with the notion that if and when photography takes a more prevalent role in my life, I will likely never return to acting.”

Sprouse Bros Brand

In the year 2005, the Cole Sprouse brothers and Dualstar Entertainment signed a licensing agreement to produce Sprouse Bros branded merchandise for the preteen and teenage market. This brand included a clothing line, a comics series, and a magazine. It also included a personal hygiene line and a line of sportswear.

Speaking about the brand, Dylan said that they wanted to “reach out to the nerd group and the cool group” and “appeal to everybody”. Dualstar and Leisure Publishing LLC launched a magazine whose target was boys. They did so in 2006 and it is called Sprouse Bros. Code.

Simon & Schuster Inc. also published two volumes of a book series titled Sprouse Bros. 47 R.O.N.I.N. It featured the Sprouse twins as “young James Bonds or undercover agents”. However, in 2008, the brothers ended their association with the Olsens’ Dualstar. Nevertheless, they continued with their clothing line. They exclusively sold the items in their clothing lines online until mid-March 2012. But the online store could no longer be accessed after that.

Controversy

Dylan was caught up in a major controversy when he sent some nude photos to a lady friend over his cellphone. Unfortunately, these photos went online and he lost his good image in the industry. Cole Sprouse stood by him in this difficult time and after this incident, the twins were shunned by the Hollywood film industry for some time.

Moreover, he spent some time off the Riverdale set and exercised his professional photography skills in Manila, the capital of the Philippines. One user commented that many of his photos “showed the real Philippines in its most vulnerable state. No pretensions, just a clear reality.” However, one of those photos and captions caught flack from fans and viewers. He shared a photo that featured an impoverished child with the caption “Well I’m not your parent, don’t gotta follow me around.”

Viewers accused the actor of sensationalizing poverty and he received mounds of negative comments on the post. With all the negative responses to the photo, he promptly apologized through an Instagram Story and one fan account captured the moment. The Instagram Story read:

“For those of you that took offense to this photo or the caption, I removed it. My apologies, and not my intention. Certainly not seeking to sensationalize poverty nor poke fun, shoulda thought more about the words used, and the implications of those words I hadn’t even considered.” Then he continues “I’ll continue to post about my travels through Manila, showing all sides of the city experiences.”
