Jay Kay wants to upgrade listed Buckinghamshire mansion

Publish date: 2024-07-27

Pop star Jay Kay has submitted plans to upgrade his luxury Grade II-listed manor house in Buckinghamshire where he keeps his fleet of cars with a bigger kitchen. 

The Jamiroquai singer, 50, has lodged a planning application for his Horsenden Manor, once valued at £4.5million, in Horsenden, near Princes Risborough.  

Submitted last December under the name 'Mrs Kay', the Space Cowboy sensation has told Wycombe District Council that he needs 'space for a modern kitchen/breakfast area, that is better suited to a family house of this size'. 

The Jamiroquai sensation has lodged a planning application for his £4.5million Horsenden Manor (pictured) in Horsenden, near Princes Risborough

The father-of-two's proposals include the addition of a glass extension with huge sliding glass doors on the back of the three-storey mansion 'to provide space for a modern kitchen/breakfast area, that is better suited to a family house of this size'. 

Jay Kay (pictured) told Wycombe District Council that he needs 'space for a modern kitchen/breakfast area, that is better suited to a family house of this size'

Formerly known as Horsenden House and built in 1810, Jay Kay's home is in a conservation area, and already boasts a salon, library, and drawing room.

The musician, who has lived just south of Aylesbury since 1995, also has a recording studio, granary, and garages for his top-end car collection.

Jay Kay's band Jamiroquai has sold more than 26 million records worldwide.

With his astonishing wealth, the singer has acquired a vast fleet of fancy cars, auctioning three supercars through Bonhams auction house in London.

A 1973 Porsche 911 Carrera RS 2.7-Litre 'Lightweight' Coupé, a 2004 Porsche Carrera GT, and a 2016 Ferrari F12tdf Berlinetta went under the hammer last year, and are believed to have raked in at least £2million from car enthusiasts.
