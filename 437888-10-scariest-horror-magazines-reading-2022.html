10 scariest horror magazines you should be reading in 2022

Publish date: 2024-08-25

A few things in the world are scary, yet they make you yearn for more. Horror literature is a perfect example as it feeds your thirst for scary content. When you buy a single issue of a horror magazine, you can be assured of sticking with it issue after issue because there is always new content every time. So which are the scariest horror magazines you should read in 2022?

Horror literature emerged in the 18th century with the Gothic novel. The focus of horror literature is to create a feeling of fear. Such tales are of ancient origin and form a substantial part of folk literature. The scariest horror stories feature supernatural elements such as ghosts, witches, or vampires. However, they can also address the more realistic psychological fears.

List of the scariest horror magazines

Over the years, you have seen many horror magazines come and go, but some have withstood the test of time. These magazines have consistently featured on the list of scary magazines. Scroll down to see some of the best horror magazines that should be on top of your reading list in 2022.

1. Rue Morgue Magazine

Top on the list of horror story magazines is Rue Morgue. The magazine was launched in 1997 by Rodrigo Gudino. Its headquarters are in Toronto, with various regional offices spread across North America, the United Kingdom and Europe. This is the world's leading horror fiction magazine distributed worldwide. Its content comprises news, reviews, and event coverage.

The magazine secured a national distribution by its fourth issue published in July 1998. It was published monthly for a few years, then from January 2015, 11 issues were published per year [no February issue]. However, from 2017 the magazine switched to bimonthly publication. The magazine won the Rondo Award in the best magazine every year from 2010- 2016.

2. Diabolique Magazine

Diabolique is one of the scary magazines that should be on top of your reading list in 2022. This digital print magazine is lavishly illustrated, exploring every aspect of horror, film, literature, and art. Each issue brings a fresh perspective into horror literature, and it brims with insightful commentary. Photos and illustrations complement the information.

The magazine was launched in 2011, and since then, its writers have been nominated for 17 Rondo Hatton Classic Horror Awards. The categories for which the magazine has been nominated include Best Magazine, Best Article, Best Cover, and Best Theme Issue. Diabolique brings back the feeling of joy and awe you experienced when you opened your first horror magazine.

3. Scream Magazine

Scream Magazine is one of the world's scariest publications. It's also the world's no. 1 print and digital full-colour magazine. The magazine has been in publication since October 2010. In 2014 scream magazine launched iScream, which allows consumers to purchase the magazine's issues digitally.

Apart from acquiring the magazine digitally, you can also access it via an online subscription. It is published bi-monthly and covers news, reviews, photos, festivals, movies, celebrity interviews, books, comics, and more. There are 100 pages per issue.

4. The Dark Magazine

Each month The Dark Magazine brings you the best in dark fantasy, weird fiction, and horror. The magazine is published by Prime Books. It is edited by award-winning editors Sean Wallace, Silvia Moreno Garcia and Michael Kelly. The magazine has been in existence for five years and is published monthly.

It has published award-winning stories by new and established authors in that short period and showcased great artwork from all corners of the world. Each month it publishes four stories that can be read for free online or bought as an e-book to be read offline.

5. Cemetery Dance

Cemetery Dance is one of the best horror short story magazines. It was founded by Richard Chizmar while he was still in college. The award-winning magazine is considered the leading publisher of horror, dark mystery and suspense in the world.

Each issue is packed with 100-140 pages of short stories, articles, columns, interviews, news and reviews. Over the years, the magazine has received press coverage in many other leading publications such as Entertainment Weekly and USA Today. It hosts top authors such as Steven King, Richard layman, and Norman Partridge.

6. Cosmic Horror Monthly

Cosmic Horror Monthly is a horror and dark fantasy magazine. It was established in the year 2020. The magazine combines classic cosmic horror with bold new weird and a vintage look and feel.

It also brings you original modern takes on cosmic horror in the form of short stories, poetry and artwork. Cosmic Horror is published electronically 12 times per year. With Cosmic Horror Monthly, you get to enjoy a monthly tome of bold new horror delivered to your inbox or your mailbox.

7. Black Static

Black Static horror magazine was founded in 1994. It was previously known as The Third Alternative before it was renamed to Black Static in 2007. The magazine is renowned for its slipstream/horror fiction and is edited by Andy Cox. It has won the British Fantasy Award for the best magazine. The individual featured stories have also won several awards.

It features renowned authors such as Nicholas Royle, Conrad Williams, Christopher Fowler, Nina Allan, and Alexander Glass. The format of the magazine changed in February 2021 to a double issue. Subsequently, the ability to purchase subscriptions was ended to wind the magazines down pending editor Andy Cox's retirement.

8. Fangoria

Fangoria is the world best horror and cult film magazine that has been in existence since 1979. At one point, it was the only place on newsstands where fans could read about the world of contemporary horror. The magazine is published four times a year by Fangoria Publishing, LLC and edited by Phil Nobile Jr.

The publication became irregular in 2015, and in December 2016, the publication stopped. In February 2018, Fangoria was bought by Cinestate Entertainment Company under the new editor in chief Phil Nobile Jr. The magazine was re-launched as a print-based quarterly publication.

From August 2018, Fangoria was acquired by the Wander Wall Entertainment CEO Tara Ansley, and entrepreneur Abhi Goel and quarterly printing continued. The new owners plan to launch a multimedia studio and produce and distribute fiction and non-fiction content.

9. The Horror Zine magazine

The list of horror journals cannot be complete without mentioning Horror Zine. Since its existence, in July 2009, the magazine has published exceptional horror fiction by emerging talent and leading authors. The magazine was set up in Sacramento by Jeani Rector, a novelist and short-story writer. She has been the editor for the magazines entire run.

Jeani selects only the most inventive and captivating stories for each issue. The magazine is published monthly, and it has won several awards, including the 2010 Pre-editors and editors best fiction and poetry magazine, the 2011 Pre-editors and editors best poetry magazine. It has also been nominated by the British fantasy society shortlist for the best magazine in 2009 and 2012.

10. Weird Tales magazine

Weird Tales is an American fantasy and horror fiction pulp magazine founded in 1922 by J. C. Henneberger and J. M. Lansinger. The magazine is regarded by historians of fantasy and science fiction as a legend in the field and is considered the most important and influential of all fantasy magazines.

It is committed to publishing brilliantly strange material that can't be found elsewhere. Over the last two decades, the magazine has featured works by such modern masters as Tanith Lee, Ramsey Campbell, Thomas Ligotti, and Nina Kiriki Hoffman.
