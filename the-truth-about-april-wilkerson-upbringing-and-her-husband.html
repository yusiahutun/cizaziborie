The Truth About April Wilkerson Upbringing And Her Husband

Publish date: 2024-04-20

April Wilkerson is well-known for sharing do-it-yourself metalworking and woodworking techniques from her home in Texas. Before deciding to start creating video tutorials, Wilkerson began her content development adventure via her blog. Both the blog and the videos were huge hits right once, which prompted a proposal from YouTube.

Over 1.3 million people subscribe to Wilkerson’s YouTube channel, and she has close to 250K Instagram followers. Even though she exhibits outstanding carpentry abilities, April claims she isn’t an expert. She sees herself as a novice and a teacher wanting to impart her expertise to her students. Due to her escapades, April now co-hosts the reality series Assembly Required with Tim Allen and Richard Karn.

The year 1987 saw the birth of April Wilkerson in Corpus Christi, Texas. She relocated to Fort Worth at the age of 19, where she remained for twelve years. April currently resides in Central Texas Hill County. She predicted that she would never leave Texas in the Call Me Mabie series. April declared, “I’ve always been in Texas and I always picture myself staying there forever.

When Wilkerson was younger, she was a skilled baton twirler before transitioning to tennis. According to her mother, April was a state, national, and world champion twirler. Wilkerson earned a degree in business management from the University of Texas at Arlington in 2012.

Before beginning her first woodworking project in January 2013, she had no prior experience. According to Wilkerson’s mother, April’s tenacity has contributed to her success to date:

She was a disaster from the beginning. independent and tough. You won’t divulge anything to her. She’s aware. Nevertheless, she exudes such sweetness and vivacity. She was wonderful.

Cody, Wilkerson’s engineer husband, doesn’t enjoy carpentry, so they don’t often collaborate on projects.

April’s enthusiasm for do-it-yourself projects grew after she and her husband Cody bought a home. April knew their kitchen needed a pantry, but she was appalled by the contractor’s $1,000 estimate. With time on her hands after graduating from college, April decided to build a pantry for her kitchen. After her accomplishment, April decided to use her blog to teach the rest of the world what she had discovered.

April Wilkerson realized that she preferred teaching over making things

When April Wilkerson decided to construct a pantry in 2013, she was a total novice. She started by borrowing a drill and a circular saw because she didn’t have any tools. She acquired her tool collection from Craigslist and pawn shops. Additionally, she salvaged and reused materials that were unwanted by customers and restaurant chains.

Even before she had finished the project, Wilkerson had made up her mind to share her experience with creating a pantry. She wanted to fill the gap in online woodworking instruction because she had found it.
