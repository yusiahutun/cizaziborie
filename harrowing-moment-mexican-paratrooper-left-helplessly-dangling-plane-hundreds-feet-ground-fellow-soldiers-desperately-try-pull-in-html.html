A Mexican paratrooper was left helplessly twisting in the air hundreds of feet above ground when he jumped into a tangle of used static lines.

In a two-minute video that captured the accident, the unidentified paratrooper can be seen caught up in the lines after jumping from the back of a plane during a military exercise.

He had been following a series of fellow paratroopers leaping out of the plane, the rest of whom were fortunate enough to have their parachutes open without catching.

The man spins for over a minute as cries to pull him back in can be heard on the audio.

Though the footage ends with the man still tethered to the plane, he was eventually, somehow, brought to safety.

AM reports that the man is recovering in the hospital, though they were unable to identify his name or the area where the incident took place.

The unidentified man is reportedly recovering at a Mexican hospital

In static-line jumps, paratroopers fall with a cord attached to the aircraft that's also fixed to the jumper's deployment bag.

As they fall, the cord eventually reaches its end and yanks open the deployment bag out, automatically releasing the chute.

Jalopnik noted that static-line parachuting was particularly effective during D-Day, and more recently during the U.S. invasion of Northern Iraq.
