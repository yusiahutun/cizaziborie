What is a digital number plate?

The government has just introduced digital number plates in Kenya, and people are trying to figure out how different the new plates are from the old ones. Digital number plates are a form of number plates with a micro-chip, allowing instant and automatic car identification.

The chip stores certain information about the vehicle. Therefore, one can access details about the vehicle instantly and efficiently.

What features does the latest number plate in Kenya have?

Implementing the digital number plate system has put Kenya on the list of countries that comply with the Vienna Traffic Conventions on global standards of number plates.

The conventions were agreed upon at the United Nations Economic and Social Council’s Conference on Road Traffic (October 7 – November 8 1968). So, what are the new features introduced in the new number plates in Kenya?

Kenya’s new-look digital number plates will have a unique identification number (serial number) tied to the chassis number of its vehicle. On top of this, the plates will have a smaller font like the ones utilized in government automobiles.

Besides the unique font, the new generation digital number plates will have a specially imprinted national flag, watermark, hologram, and serial numbers for rear and front plates.

The chip on the digital number plate will store various information. The data includes the vehicle's year of manufacture, type and colour, engine number, date and place of manufacture, transmission type and insurance details.

How to apply for a new number plate in Kenya: A step-by-step guide

The Cabinet Secretary for Interior Security, among other government officials, launched the digital number plate in Kenya on August 30, 2022, at the GSU Recce unit Headquarters in Ruiru. During the launch, they stated that the shift was being done by a multi-agency unit of the security sector, continuing with the reforms initiated by President Uhuru Kenyatta after the 2019 Dusit attack.

And they laid down the procedure car owners must take to have the digital number plate installed on their cars. It is important to note that all motorists must acquire an application from the National Transport and Safety Authority (NTSA). The form will have all the details of the vehicle.

Of course, the easiest way to do this is through a TIMS account that you can access through the official website. Once you have opened the website, log into your account, and use the following steps to put in a request for the latest number plate in Kenya.

  • Head to the number plate application section in your TIMS account.
  • Select the automobile that requires the digital number plate. You will notice a list if you have more than one vehicle.
  • Choose the type of plate you desire and who will pick them. You can also indicate where you want to collect the number plate from (your desired delivery destination).
  • After stating your location, you will get a prompt to pay for the service via mobile money (M-Pesa or Airtel Money) or card.
  • Once you have paid, you will get an SMS on your phone informing you when your digital number plate will be ready.

It is important to note that the application fee for the digital number plate is Ksh.3,000. An applicant must also submit Ksh.700 to replace the third license. Anyone replacing or registering for a motorcycle number plate will pay Ksh.1,500. People looking for a preferred registration number are required to pay Ksh.30,000.

When is the deadline for getting digital plates in Kenya?

The Cabinet Secretary for the Ministry of Interior and Coordination of National Government, Fred Matiang’I, mentioned that Kenyans would have 18 months to comply with getting the digital number plates.

People can start replacing their old number plates from October 1, 2022. Therefore, if you count 18 months from the date, the deadline will be Monday, April 1, 2024.

Number plate colours with meaning in Kenya

There are five different number plates in Kenya issued by the Registrar of Motor Vehicles for civilians, the Government of Kenya, parastatals, local authorities, and diplomats. Some of these number plates will have different colours from the common civilian ones.

Diplomatic number plates are usually red, while motorbikes and TukTuks are given four letters in yellow. All public vehicles' number plates are generally yellow from behind and white from the front. Number plates for government institutions and parastatals are blue.
