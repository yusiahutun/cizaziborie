Inside enchanting five-bed 'Style Cottage' in Surrey

Publish date: 2024-05-03

A charming five-bedroom cottage in Surrey has entered the market for £1,750,000.

The Grade II listed home, called Style Cottage, is thought to have originally been a pair of Hall Houses in the 16th century. It was then extended in the 20th century to become the family home it is today.

The property features beautiful wall and ceiling beams throughout, with oak flooring and a number of working fireplaces. There's also a space downstairs, which is being used as a wine cellar by the current owners.

The cottage benefits from four bathrooms and four bedrooms, complete with original period features that add an abundance of character. One particular highlight is the visible smoke bay in one of the bathrooms.

Outside the cottage, you'll find a two-storey barn with lots of potential for a guest house, studio or home office, subject to planning consent.

At the front of the house, there's a triple car barn-style garage, with a gravel driveway and lawn. At the back of the property, there's a private terrace.

On the southern side of the property, there's a paddock that extends down to the River Wey with a frontage of over 170 metres.

Potential buyers should note that Style Cottage is freehold, but the field is leasehold, for which a peppercorn rent is payable.

Location-wise, Style Cottage is conveniently accessible to the A3 with lots of surrounding schools, farm shops and garages making it the ideal family base. For a greater range of shops and leisure amenities, the town of Guildford is just six miles away.
