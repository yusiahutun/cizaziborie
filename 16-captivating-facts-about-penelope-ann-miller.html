Rising to Fame

Penelope Ann Miller, born on January 13, 1964, in Los Angeles, California, is an incredibly talented actress who has made a name for herself in the entertainment industry.

Early Film Performances

Prior to her breakout role, Miller appeared in films like “Adventures in Babysitting” (1987) and “Big Top Pee-wee” (1988).

Iconic Role in “Carlito’s Way”

One of her most memorable performances came in the 1993 crime drama “Carlito’s Way,” where she starred alongside Al Pacino.

Award Nominations

Miller’s exceptional performances have earned her numerous award nominations, including a Golden Globe nomination for Best Supporting Actress.

Versatility in Acting

With her incredible range, Miller has effortlessly transitioned between drama, comedy, and even period films.

Broadway Debut

Miller showcased her theatrical talent by making her Broadway debut in the acclaimed play “Our Town” in 1988.

Notable Television Roles

Alongside her film career, Miller has also made impactful appearances on television shows such as “CSI: NY” and “American Crime.

Working with Hollywood Legends

Throughout her career, Miller has had the privilege of working with legendary actors like Robert De Niro, Sean Penn, and Marlon Brando.

Personal Life

Off-screen, Miller married actor Will Arnett, and they have two children together.

Philanthropic Efforts

Miller actively supports organizations like the Juvenile Diabetes Research Foundation and the Pediatric Epilepsy Project.

Regaining Prominence

In recent years, Miller has experienced a career resurgence with notable roles in films such as “The Artist” (2011) and “American Crime: The People v. O.J. Simpson” (2016).

Recognitions
