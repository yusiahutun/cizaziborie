8 Dog Breeds Loved by The Royal family

Publish date: 2024-06-24
1

Corgis

Bettmann//Getty Images

The Queen is often associated with her love of Corgis, supposedly owning over 30 Pembroke Welsh Corgi dogs during her reign. They were also owned by her parents, King George VI and Queen Elizabeth The Queen Mother.

Pictured: Queen Elizabeth II of England at Balmoral Castle with one of her Corgis, 28th September 1952.

2

Dorgis

Steve Parsons//Getty Images

A Dorgi — a cross between a Dachshund and Welsh Corgi – started when one of the Queen's Corgis mated with a Dachshund. Loyal, friendly and intelligent, these pups are incredibly sweet.

3

Cocker Spaniel

Max Mumby/Indigo//Getty Images

Lupo was an English Cocker Spaniel owned by Prince William and Catherine, the Duke and Duchess of Cambridge. He sadly passed away in November 2020 after many happy years with the family.

More recently, the Queen was also given a Cocker Spaniel named Lissy, proving that her love for dogs goes far beyond her adored Corgis.

Pictured: Prince William, Duke of Cambridge carries his dog Lupo at the The Golden Metropolitan Polo Club Charity Cup polo match.

4

King Charles Spaniel

Central Press//Getty Images

Princess Margaret wanted something different than the Corgis so loved by her sister, so she got her own King Charles Spaniel named Rowley.

Pictured: Princess Margaret and her then fiancé, Antony Armstrong-Jones, stroking their pet King Charles Spaniel, at the Royal Lodge, Windsor.

5

Jack Russell Terrier

Tim Graham//Getty Images

Prince Charles is a longtime owner of Jack Russell terriers, loving the breed for many years. As well as having them when he was growing up, Prince Charles and the Duchess of Cornwall have two adorable rescue Jack Russell Terriers named Bluebell and Beth.

Pictured: Prince Charles With His Jack Russell Terrier Pet Dog At Smiths Lawn Polo Ground Windsor Circa 1970s

6

Labrador Retrievers

Lisa Sheridan//Getty Images

King George VI owned a number of Labradors as gun dogs and pets. According to Royal.uk, he was particularly fond of yellow Labradors, which were still relatively uncommon in the early twentieth century.

Pictured: George VI with his dogs in the grounds of the Royal Lodge, Windsor, June 1936

7

Bull Terriers

Max Mumby/Indigo//Getty Images

Princess Anne has been caring for English Bull Terriers for decades, although her pups are not always known for being well behaved.

Pictured: Princess Anne walks her Bull Terrier dogs as she attends the Gatcombe Horse Trials at Gatcombe Park

8

Norfolk Terriers

Drew Angerer//Getty Images

Princess Beatrice and Princess Eugenie are both fond owners of the adorable Norfolk Terriers. In fact, the York family owner four Terriers Jack, Cici, Teddy, and Ginger.
