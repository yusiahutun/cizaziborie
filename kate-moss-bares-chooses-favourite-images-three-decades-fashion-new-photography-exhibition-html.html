Kate Moss naked in her favourite images from her three decades in fashion

Publish date: 2024-09-21

She's been modelling for top photographers for 27 years.

But amongst the millions of photographs taken of her, Kate Moss has chosen her 11 favourites.

The Croydon-born model's top picks are a racy selection, with several nude images taken by the likes of Mario Testino and Annie Leibovitz picked by the model back in 2011 for the Kate Moss Portfolio, and for the first time they are exhibited in a gallery in the UK. 

Scroll down for video 

Revealing all: Kate Moss has chosen her favourite ever images of herself for the Kate Moss Portfoilio, with the collection now on show in a UK gallery for the first time

Kate, 41, went back through the archives to choose images for the Portfolio, produced in collaboration with James Danziger of Danziger Projects (NY), with the photographs now on show at the Osborne Samuel and Beetles + Huxley's annual collaborative exhibition in London.

There's more natural images too, including a candid 1997 snap by Bruce Weber of the young star, who was scouted aged 14 at JFK airport in New York, blowing bubbles, again while in the nude. 

Perhaps the most famous portrait in the exhibition is one of Kate's first Calvin Klein images, for the 1993 Obsession campaign which Kate has previously confirmed was based on then-boyfriend Mario Sorrenti's own obsession with her.

Speaking to photographer and friend Nick Knight earlier this year, Kate explained of the famous shots: 'I'd wake up in the morning and he'd be taking pictures of me and I'd be like "F**k off" and I laid like that for about 10 days. He would not stop taking pictures of that.' 

Iconic: Perhaps the most famous portrait in the exhibition is one of Kate's first Calvin Klein images, for the 1993 Obsession campaign, shot by her then-boyfriend Mario Sorrenti

Kicking back: Juergen Teller shot a then-26-year-old Kate smoking in front of a roaring fire

Speaking one-to-one with Knight, who she's worked with for 20 years, for ShowStudio.com the supermodel described her reluctance to be constantly photographed and how Mario treating her as his muse lead to the downfall of their relationship.

'10 days?!' The model continued. 'We split up after that. When you're in a relationship with a photographer and they start abusing that relationship by being like, "I want you to do this" it makes you like, "No".'

'I didn't want to work all the time. He'd be like, "Get up on the roof, take your clothes off" and I'd be like "F**k off! I don't want to do that".

'Now I understand that kind of thing a bit better, capturing an image and working a lot. But at the time I was 17.' 

Osborne Samuel and Beetles + Huxley are bringing together over 150 prints by leading artists spanning from 1910 to the present day for their 2015 exhibition which will be spread across both London galleries.

The Photographers 2015 exhibition will trace the history of photography from its early developments through its rise in status as both creative and documentary medium, including The Kate Moss Portfolio by Mario Testino, Annie Leibovitz, Terry Richardson and Bruce Weber. 

The Photographers 2015 runs at Osborne Samuel and Beetles + Huxley galleries, London from November 25 to December 23.
