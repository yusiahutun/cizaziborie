Shocking moment man fatally stabs his mother in a Texas convenience mart

Publish date: 2024-09-28

Alejandro Sanabria, 26, is being held in the Angelina County Jail on $6 million bond after fatally stabbing his  other and injuring a police officer during a manic episode

Shocking video footage shows the terrifying moment a son fatally stabbed his mother as they waited in line at a Texas convenience store last Friday.

Martha Hageon, 48, died after being taken off life support on Tuesday. 

Her 26-year-old son Alejandro Sanabria had attacked her in the Lucky's Convenience Store in Lufkin at around 10.30pm Friday while he was in a manic state due to his bipolar disorder. 

Hageon had been trying to keep her son away from his pregnant partner and child until his manic episode, which was then in its third day, was over, when he turned on her, according to the Lufkin Daily News. 

Sanabria was dragged away from his mother and detained by officers who had just entered the store to carry out a welfare check on Hageon after she texted her husband to call 911 because her son was acting 'crazy'.

Officer Gerardo Salinas, 29, was also stabbed in the side and suffered a punctured lung as cops tried to wrestle a large knife away from Sanabria. 

He is now recovering at home after being released from hospital on Monday. 

Police were first made aware that Hageon may be in danger after receiving a 911 call from her husband, Jimmie, in which he asked them to check in on her and her son.

She had allegedly texted saying that she needed cops to pull her car over.

Martha Hageon, 48, died after being taken off life support on Tuesday. She is pictured above with her 26-year-old son Alejandro Sanabria who she was trying to protect during an episode

As cops approached the store, Sanabria pulled out a large knife and stabbed his mother

Bodycam footage showed the officers wrestling the knife from his hands

'My wife just called me telling me to call 911. I am heading that way now. I do not know what is going on. She said call 911. She needs them to pull her car over,' he tells the dispatcher in the newly released audio.

'Probably has her son with her. Her son's acting silly. He is acting crazy or something. I do not know what's going on.'

He said that when he received his wife's text, he called her, and she screamed at him to call the police.

'I don't know what's going on, I'm just trying to find my wife,' he tells the dispatcher, as he gives information on her car so officers can track it down.

The distressed husband explains that he is driving around trying to find his wife's car as he had no further details on where she had been headed.

He had last seen her before he left for work at 11am that morning, he said.

Police had been alerted to Hageon's danger after she texted her husband to tell him to call 911 and officers tracked her down. She is pictured above with her husband, Jimmie

The stabbing took place in this Lucky's store in Lufkin on Friday night

Surveillance footage shows Hageon and Sanabria waiting in line at the store

He begins to hug her and kisses her on the head as they wait

Yet when he notices officers approaching, Sanabria turned violent and pulled out a knife

The dispatcher says officers are also on the lookout and asks Jimmie to call back again if he hears from his wife.

One and a half minutes later, Jimmie calls for a second time and says Hageon told him she was at the Lucky's store.

'He is going crazy. Her son is going crazy. I need to get my wife out of that car,' he pleads with the dispatcher.

Before police arrived, surveillance footage from the store showed Hageon and Sanabria standing together in line behind other customers.

Hageon, wearing a black top and jeans, can be seen looking anxiously at her son before trying to step away from him to the other side of the store.

Sanabria, in a yellow hoodie, is shown in the video waving his arm and appearing to shout out before pointing to the ceiling.

His mother speaks to him and takes their purchases from his hand as she anxiously looks around.

Bodycam footage shows officers wrestling Sanabria to the ground in the store

They pile on top of him and they wrench the knife from his hands

As he was taken into custody, Sanabria was covered in blood, as pictured

Her son wraps his arm around her neck and places his head on hers in what looks like an embrace but Hageon begins to look distressed and raises her hand to try and pull free.

They both look to the door, where officers are just beginning to approach the store, and Sanabria suddenly pulls a large knife from his pocket.

He pulls his mother back and holds it toward her while looking toward the door and the approaching cops before the footage ends.

In the bodycam video from the responding officers, they are seen rushing through the door of the store and tackling Sanabria as they try to kick the knife from his hand.

Officer Gerardo Salinas, 29, pictured above, also suffered a punctured lung as he made the arrest. He has been released from hospital and is recovering at home

He struggles and tries to hold on to his mother, before the officers pin him to the ground and wrestle the knife away.

As Sanabria is brought from the store, the bodycam shows his clothing covered in blood.

Hageon was taken to hospital to be treated for her injuries but died four days later.

Sanabria is currently being held in the Angelina County Jail on $6 million bond on charges of aggravated assault with a deadly weapon against a public servant and aggravated assault with a deadly weapon.

Police said the charges would be updated in the coming days.

His uncle Peter Maldonado told the Lufkin Daily News that because of his condition, Sanabria may not yet realize what he has done to his mother, as he could still be in the manic episode.

Maldonado described Sanabria as a 'loving, caring, funny and thoughtful guy'. 

Maldonado says he hopes his nephew, who was diagnosed with a bipolar disorder three years ago, will be brought to a hospital.

'Unfortunately, insurance is a barrier with seeking (mental health) help,' he said. 'Because he is 26, he's no longer covered by his father's insurance and finding him resources for treatment was challenging.'

Martha Hageon, pictured, died Tuesday after being taken off life support

Her husband Jimmie, pictured together above, had tried to get cops to her in time

Hageon, 48, worked as a social worker for the Texas Health & Human Services Commission

Maldonado explained that help cannot be forced on Sanabria now he is an adult and his condition left him believing that everyone was after him.

'He could be seen walking around and doing pushups, talking fast, having grandiose ideas about life coupled with paranoia,' Maldonado said. 

'He thought people were reading his mind or his thoughts. I've seen him in front of a TV — he thought the actors on a TV show were reading his thoughts; that's how we knew he was in a manic state.'

Sanabria has been arrested multiple times including one arrest for assault causing bodily injury in 2012 when he was also in a manic state.

According to her obituary, Hageon was a San Antonio native and a social worker for the Texas Health & Human Services Commission.

She had two other sons, Alberto, and Arturo Sanabria.
