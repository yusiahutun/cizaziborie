Kicking up a stink: Airline branded 'family haters' after couple are left stranded while changing baby's nappy

By Daily Mail Reporter
Updated: 12:07 EDT, 9 November 2010

View
comments


Any new parent will be familiar with the situation - you're in a rush but your child's nappy just has to be changed.

That was the scenario in which Dan Blais and Colleen Roberge found themselves, only multiplied by ten - they were racing to get on a plane.

The Canadian couple were with their eight-month-old son Levi at the boarding gate of Las Vegas Airport when they had no choice but pay a visit to the baby changing room.

Fighting the airline: Dan Blais and his wife Collen Roberge with baby son Levi after their ordeal at Las Vegas Airport

On their return, an airline worker from Alaska Airlines had given one of their tickets away, leaving them stranded hundreds of miles from their St. Albert home in Canada, according to the Edmonton Journal.

And their luggage, particularly the supplies to keep little Levi happy, had left with the plane.

Now the couple have set up a blog called 'Alaska Air Hates Families' and a Twitter page in a bid recoup they $1,000 they lost to the unbending airline.

The family were forced to stay an extra night and buy new tickets to get home the following day.

Blais and Roberge, making their first trip with their young son, had been married over the Thanksgiving weekend and had enjoyed a wonderful time.

That day, they were scheduled to fly back to Edmonton through Seattle with Alaska Airlines at 3.15pm.

Mr Blais said they arrived at the gate 20 minutes before take-off, but Levi chose a very unfortunate moment to fill his nappy.

Scene of the diaper disaster: Las Vegas airport in Nevada, USA

'It turned out to be one of those big messes,' he said. 'The kind for which a diaper proves wholly inadequate'

His wife rushed to the bathroom to deal with Levi, while at the gate, Blais told the agent what happened.

Mr Blais complained: 'Basically she said, "I'll give you two minutes" - and if they weren't back they'd lose their seat.

Blais ran to the bathroom and yelled for his wife to hurry. Inside, she scrambled to get Levi's nappy changed and clothes back on. But when she came out, it was too late. The agent had given away her seat.

Ms Roberge, who questioned the woman worker at the time of the incident , said: '(She) basically said to us you should get on the plane with the contents of your baby's diaper running down his legs.'

'It's like: "We don't care. Just get on".

'It was a terrible, stressful experience. But I'm not asking for any emotional damages. I just want them to pay me back for what it cost me to get home.'

A spokesman for Alaska Airlines said the couple were late for boarding and added: 'If we accommodate people who arrive late, we risk arriving at the destination late. That's not fair to everyone else who boarded on time.'
