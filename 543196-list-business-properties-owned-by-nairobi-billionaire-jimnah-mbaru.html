Nairobi billionaire Jimnah Mbaru is moving the tables behind the scenes.

Mbaru is now new in the Kenyan business sector as his entrepreneurial journey started in the 1970s when he quit civil service and studied for a master of business administration (MBA).

Since then, he has embarked on a journey that has established him as one of the pioneers in the field of finance, setting a standard that will inspire generations to come.

“The language of money is an interesting one. It is mostly a silent one. If you rush into it, you might misunderstand it. It is a language of patience, studying, and predicting,” the former Nairobi Securities Exchange (NSE) chairman said, as quoted by Capital FM.

What was Jimnah Mbaru's first business?

According to Business Daily, He started honing his business acumen in primary school. He dealt with scrap metal when he was in elementary school to supplement his income.

Upon enrolling in Kirogo Secondary in 1963, he became involved in the fast-moving goods industry, which was more profitable and well-liked by his contemporaries.

“In high school, I traded in scones, buying 15 for a shilling and then selling each for ten cents. I would make 50 per cent profit. As a student at the university, I used to trade in the NSE and also lend students money," he revealed.

Which businesses do Mbaru have an interest in?

1. Britam

According to Business Today, Mbaru made KKSh 318 million in June 2023 when he sold 64.8 million shares of his holdings in the financial services company Britam, listed on the NSE.

Notably, in 2019, Mbaru sold 50 million Britam shares to Swiss Re, a reinsurer with its headquarters in Zurich.

During the same year, he re-established his ownership stake in the company at 198.4 million after buying the same number of shares on the open market.

2. Dyer & Blair Investment Bank

Mbaru is the chairman and chief executive officer of Dyer & Blair Investment Bank. He acquired it from Kenya Commercial Bank at KSh 400,000 after the original owner defaulted on a loan.

The financial company engages in regional investment banking, securities, investment management, and other services.

The 76-year-old businessman has over 30 years of investment banking and financial sector experience.

It was one of the founding members of the Rwanda Stock Exchange (RSE) in 2011, the Nairobi Securities Exchange (NSE), and the Uganda Securities Exchange (USE) in 1997.

The NSE 17 Share Index was compiled by Dyer & Blair for about 25 years beginning in 1966, and it was eventually taken over by the NSE Secretariat in 1990.

3. Transcentury Group

Mbaru was the founding member of Transcentury Group, an investment firm. As of May 28, 2007, Mbaru ranked as Transcentury's sixth-largest shareholder.

In 2015, the tycoon sold 9.4 million shares (3.3%) he held in the company.

As a result, he dropped from the list of the top 10 shareholders.
