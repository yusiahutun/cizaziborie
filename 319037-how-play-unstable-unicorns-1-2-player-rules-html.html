How to play Unstable Unicorns: 1 and 2-player rules

Publish date: 2024-02-20

Are you looking for a fun game to keep you busy and entertained? There are lots of strategic, board, and card games you can opt for, from checkers to scrabble, chess, and more. But how about a revolutionary, fun game that will keep you engaged for hours? This is exactly what Unstable Unicorns offers you. But do you know how to play Unstable Unicorns?

Unstable Unicorns is a strategic card game that takes a different and fun approach by giving two amazing gaming features –destruction and unicorns. In this game, you face off with your friends by building a powerful army of unicorns while doing your best to make sure they don't build theirs. Below is a detailed look at how to play Unstable Unicorns, from the rules of the game to tips for winning and other important details.

An overview of Unstable Unicorns

Never played Unstable Unicorns before? Don't worry. The game is fairly simple, even for first-timers. The objective of the game is to build a strong army of unicorns, with the first person to build the army winning the game. There is a lot of shuffling during the gameplay, but unlike most strategic games, Unstable Unicorns is full of humour, not to mention that you will be staring at very adorable unicorns.

How to play Unstable Unicorns

Never played Unstable Unicorns before? Below is a look at how to play:

1. Game components

The Unstable Unicorns card games are made up of 104 cards. These are:

  • 30 magical unicorn cards
  • 23 magic cards
  • 22 basic unicorn cards
  • 14 upgrade cards
  • 14 instant cards
  • 13 baby unicorn cards
  • 8 downgrade cards

Aside from these cards, the game also comes with a rule book, which guides the players on how to proceed. The objective and rules change depending on the number of players involved.

2. Unstable Unicorns rules

Below is a look at the Unstable Unicorns rules for 1 player and 2 players' scenarios:

a) Unstable Unicorns 1 player rules

While the game is designed to be played by two or more players, you can still play it on your own. The objective is, however, completely different from a multi-player version of the game. As a single-player, you aim to destroy as many unicorns as possible all the while avoiding off-limit Neigh cards.

At the end of the game, your points are compounded based on the number of unicorn cards remaining (in the Hunting Grounds) and the Neigh cards you have destroyed (in the Graveyard). The more the cards, the more points you lose.

For a single-player setup, you will need:

  • 22 Basic Unicorn cards
  • 30 Magical Unicorn cards
  • 8 Neigh cards
  • A coin for flipping

To set the game up:

  • Start the game by shuffling the Basic Unicorn and Neigh Cards and then lay them on the table/ground with the faces down. Using the cards, create the Hunting Grounds by placing the cards in 5 rows, each with six cards. (The Hunting Grounds is the action zone, where you will attempt to destroy the unicorn cards.)
  • Create your deck by shuffling the Magical Unicorn cards and setting them aside
  • Designate spots on the surface for the Graveyard (where the destroyed cards go), the Effect Chamber (this is where Chamber cards are. Only one card at a time, and thus you must discard the card already in the chamber if you draw a new one), and the Discard Pile (this is where the deck or chamber cards go after discarding them)

Here's the play mode:

Once the set-up is complete, you can start playing. The game is played in turns, with each turn having 4 phases:

  • The beginning: where you perform the action required by the card at the start of a turn
  • The action: where you reveal or destroy a card in the Hunting Grounds
  • The draw: where you draw a card from the deck
  • The end: where you perform the action required by the card

    • The game ends when you run out of deck cards. At this point, you should tally your points as follows:

    • Add one point for every Basic Unicorn card and remove 2 points for every Neigh Card in the Graveyard
    • Add 2 points for every Neigh card and remove 1 point for every Basic Unicorn card in the hunting grounds

    • Gather two Neigh cards and give one to each player
    • Shuffle the rest of the cards and give each 5 to each player. The rest of the cards will go to the draw pile, facing down
