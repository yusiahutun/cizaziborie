Wanjiku Chebet Kanjumba: Kenya's First Astronaut Working at NASA, a US Space Academy Graduate

Publish date: 2024-07-07

Wanjiku Chebet Kanjumba is a Kenyan Astronaut Engineer and Kenya's first Scientist-Astronaut candidate program graduate from the Advanced PoSSUM Space Academy, USA.

Born and raised in Kenya, she holds a Master of Science in Aerospace, Aeronautical and Astronautical Engineering.

She also holds a Bachelor of Science (BSc) in Aerospace, Aeronautical and Astronautical Engineering from Embry-Riddle Aeronautical University, which she termed as a dream come true.

Her LinkedIn profile indicates she is experienced in Technical and Analytical Skills, Geographic Information System (GIS) operations, Percipient Personal Strengths, Computer-Aided Design (CAD), Computer-Aided Engineering (CAE), Finite Element Analysis (FEA), Teamwork, Leadership, Problem Solving, and Spacecraft and Satellite Design.

Speaking during an interview with Space in Africa, the 25-year-old said she was interested in science and learning about how the world worked and objects from outer space from a young age.

"So, I wanted to combine my passion for learning about space and how things work – and the answer was Aerospace Engineering," she said.

Determined to realise her dream, she joined Embry-Riddle Aeronautical University to do her Bachelor’s and Masters in Aerospace Engineering.

"I applied to the program and got accepted because of my technical background," she said.

Project PoSSUM was relatively new at the time, and not many people had graduated from their academy, and it just happened that she was the first Kenyan to be accepted to the program.

Besides her work, Chebet runs her own company known as Vicillion, which on the outside, looks like a typical consulting company, but it is structured differently; it is a technology research and development lab.

"We are avid problem-solvers – as long as we are imperfect as a species, there will always be problems to solve or improve on, and we are leveraging that fact," she said, adding that she has never been interested in general employment.

However, she had the chance to be employed full-time in one of the top airlines in the United States, where she worked with the engineering department to handle their fleets’ maintenance and aircraft performance.

The overall experience was enjoyable for the young lady as she could fly anywhere around the United States without paying for a ticket, and I also visited various tourist sites.

"I worked with people in different age brackets in my department, so I analysed diverse perspectives. Although the experience was great, I realised that it was not meant for me," she said."I didn’t see the point, personally, of working for any corporation my whole life, where I would not be able to impact people’s lives and engage in endeavours that matter to me", she added.

She started developing some ideas, but she couldn’t get the necessary funds. Then, she met someone with a similar experience – not having enough funds to actualise his plans and lacking the required technical expertise to execute them.

She pitched the concept of Vicillion to him, and they combined their resources and started working on the company together.

Vicillion is still in its infancy; it was incorporated in February 2021 and started recruiting its decentralised network of agents in April.

Aside from herself, co-founders, advisors, lab directors, and VPs, their talent pool exceeds 30 professionals from various corners of the world on all six continents.

It saddens my heart every time I think about that.

But, as a species, we can accomplish amazing things when we work together, irrespective of our background or status.

Kanjumba is a firm believer that we can all learn something from each other.

She intends to use Vicillion as that inspiration to make every woman believe that nothing is impossible, especially when they set their mind to it.

The scientist promised to create platforms to encourage young women to engage in STEM programs in the future.

Pursuing her education has not been free of obstacles.

"Studying in the United States is very expensive, and I was supported mainly by my widowed mother; my father died from cancer when I was 12," she said.

She had to sacrifice a lot to educate Kanjumba, and there were times when things were challenging when she did not have enough money for food or was not sure she would even graduate.

"I am also working on my own company, aiming to write my name in the sands of time," she said.

Presently, she is working on Vicillion full-time because the idea is that once Vicillion can operate without the need of its founders, they will take a step back from the operations to focus on some other projects.

She also wants to do her PhD in Japan, where her dissertation will be focused on one of her projects at Vicillion.

If you live with limitations on yourself, you won’t accomplish much, but the world is your oyster if you put no limits," she said."There’s nothing worse than living in regret, so take a chance on yourself; you are capable of a lot more than you think," adised the 25-year-old.
