Jen Hale- Professional Career

Jen Hale began her career in news and television while still in college. She began working for CNN as an intern in their Washington, D.C. bureau. Jen began her professional career as a political reporter for KNOE-TV, which assigned her to Baton Rouge as their capital correspondent. She went on to work as a weekend anchor and reporter for WVTM in Birmingham, Alabama. Jen conducted interviews with retired athletes and notable visitors at LSU football games for their website. The New Orleans Saints organization recommended her to Fox Sports after enjoying her work with the team.

Jen wrote a book called Historic Plantations of Alabama’s Black Belt in 2009, in which she investigates the history of seventeen of Alabama’s best plantation homes. Jen worked as a Sideline Reporter for TNT’s coverage of the 2017 NBA Playoffs’ first round. Due to COVID-19, she was the lone reporter covering the Basketball Tournament in Columbus, Ohio for ESPN in 2020.
