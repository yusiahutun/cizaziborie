'Cruel trolls say disabled husband cant satisfy me in bedroom theyre wrong'

Publish date: 2024-08-30

A couple hit back at keyboard warriors who make cruel comments about their “interabled” relationship.

Hannah Aylward is happily married to Shane Burcaw, who was born with spinal muscular atrophy (SMA).

The genetic condition affects the nerves controlling muscle movement – so muscles become weak and waste away.

So Shane, from the US, has used a wheelchair since he was two-years-old.

This hasn’t stopped him finding love – but the pair have to put up with ignorant comments from trolls.

In a YouTube video titled “laughing at your ableist BS”, Hannah said: “Often people will assume that I’m Shane’s sister or mum.”

“People say that I couldn’t possibly be satisfied, we get that every day.”

Shane says people often assume he is unable to have sex – but this isn’t true.

He said: “They perceive that I can’t have sex in their very limited, missionary man f***s woman. They think that is the be all and end all of sex.

"Little do they know…"

Shane added: "Our intimacy benefits from my disability and our intimacy is not just me enjoying it.

“It doesn’t look like what you see in the movies but I can totally do it.”

Shane is often the one to initiate intimacy and communicates openly with Hannah so they both enjoy their time in the bedroom.

He explained: “I just say ‘hey, do you wanna go have sex?’

“I know that that probably shakes people as like ‘oh that must be not romantic’ but take our word for it. After I ask that, we are romantic.”

Shane continued: “When we are in bed together I can ask her ‘will you roll me towards you’ or ‘can you move my arm so I can reach your neck or cheek’.”

And Hannah added: “In general Shane uses his voice in ways that other people might just physically do something.

“We have shorthand for all of these things – he doesn’t say ‘can you move my legs under your legs’, we just know.

“I feel like I know his body and preferences as well as I know my own.”

The video has garnered more than 312,000 views and the couple were praised for speaking so candidly about their sex life.

One commenter said: “This couple is inspirational…not because of Shane’s disabilities, but because of the way they love, respect and communicate with each other. I just love them!”

Another said: “I have followed Shane and Hannah for years. My husband is also in a wheelchair, and it's so nice to see other interabled couples have deep meaningful love.”

And a third added: “They are the most real couple there is. Shane has never been a burden and he has supported her emotional needs. So glad they were featured on this program.”
