Chris Hayes Biography and Age

TV presenter Chris Hayes is of Italian and Irish descent and was born Christopher Loffredo Hayes. He was born on February 28, 1979, in the Bronx, New York. Together with his two other brothers, he grew up in New York with his parents, Roger and Geri Hayes. For his education, Hayes attended Hunter College High School in New York, and from there he received a Bachelor of Arts degree in Philosophy from Brown University.

Soon after completing his education, Chris joined the Chicago Reader newspaper, where he covered political stories. From there he moved to the monthly In These Times, with which he spent the next 4 years. He continued to write until 2010 when he joined MSNBC and started to work as a guest presenter of some shows.

Once a fellow of the Bernard Schwartz and Edmond J. Safra Foundation Center for Ethics, Chris Hayes began hosting his shows on MSNBC in 2011, which became known as Up with Chris Hayes. In 2013 he left the show for All In with Chris Hayes, while Ed Schultz, who had previously hosted the show, switched to weekends.

The move was significant in many ways, as apart from being more responsible, he was the youngest person to host a prime-time show for the major cable news channels.

Hayes has published many articles in various major magazines and newspapers in the country, although he is still the editor of The Nation magazine. In addition to his work as a television show host, Hayes is also a writer. He published his first work, Twilight of the Elites: America After Meritocracy in 2002 and his second, A Colony in a Nation in 2017. Although there were not many reviews of his second work, the first was described by some critics as both convincing and provocative.
