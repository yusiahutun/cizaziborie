Germany in September: Weather, Tips &amp; More

Publish date: 2024-03-02

September is the best time to visit Germany if you love settled weather and want to miss the summer crowds. It’s also when some top German events happen. Little wonder why this month remains the height of the country’s peak season. 

The weather in Germany in September is warm and presents the perfect opportunity for enjoying numerous outdoor activities. You’ll also have a nice time exploring posh locations like Munich, Cologne, Hamburg, etc., when you travel to Germany in September.

Germany Weather in September

Generally, the average high in September is 67 degrees Fahrenheit (19 degrees Celsius). The average low is 49 degrees Fahrenheit (9 degrees Celsius. However, the temperature in Germany in September largely depends on the city you’re in. You should also note that it can be drizzly and cold on bad weather days.

The country’s capital, Berlin, has similar temperatures to Munich, home of Oktoberfest. These locations experience average daily highs and lows of 66°F (19°C) and 52°F (11°C). Meanwhile, in the southwest, Frankfurt has an average daily high of 70°F (21°C) and a low of 54°F (12°C). The table below details Germany’s weather in September.

Why Visit Germany in September?

One of the best reasons to visit Germany in September is the amazing weather. The weather in Germany in September is warm and sunny on most days. However, it’s not as humid and hot as one will find it to be in July and August. You’ll also enjoy snow-free alpine paths when you visit Germany during this month.

In summary, the summer-autumn transition that this month presents makes it a great time to visit Germany. Here are other reasons why you should travel to Germany in September:

Longest Daylight Hours 

If you love to experience Germany in the daytime and don’t fancy night activity,  September is the best month to visit. That’s because this is the month when you enjoy some of the longest daylight hours. Germany experiences about 12 hours of daylight in September.

No Summer Crowds

Do you want to visit Germany in its peak season but want to escape the crowd as much as possible? Then, September is the best time to visit. Germany’s peak season for tourism is May-September. So, you can expect the summer crowd to be leaving and fewer people traveling for vacations at this time.

Festivals/Events

If you’re visiting Germany for festivals and exciting events, you better plan your trip for September. Yes, there’s almost always a festival in Germany every month. However, no one is as big as Oktoberfest, the bucket-list festival which typically happens during the second half of September.

Other festivals in Germany in September include the Berlin international literature festival, cannstatter volksfest in Stuttgart, Berlin beer week, Rheingau wine festival in Frankfurt, etc.

Where to Go and What to Do

The best places to visit in Germany in September depend on numerous factors like your interests, budget, etc.  For example, if you are in Germany for nightlife, the small City of Hamburg is your best bet for an amazing time. Meanwhile, Munich is a perfect choice if you want to experience beautiful culture and architecture.

Generally, there are a lot of museums, castles, historic royal residences, etc., in various locations across the country. Visit Cologne if you enjoy going to religious centers and pilgrimage sites, art galleries, cafes, and boutiques. Meanwhile, Leipzig is famous for its markets and traditional trade fairs. Top tourist attractions to visit in Germany include Fernsehturm in Berlin, Heidelberger Schloss, the entire town of Rothenburg ob der Tauber, Brandenburg Gate, etc.

There are also numerous things to do in Germany. You can watch river surfing in Eisenbach, Munich, enjoy hiking adventures in Altmuhltal Nature Park, tour the historic city of Berlin, etc.

Final Thoughts

Do you want to visit Germany in September? If yes, you’re making the right decision, as September is one of the best months to visit this country. Although the temperature in Germany in September drops a little from the July/August highs, it remains a popular time to visit Germany due to its warm weather. 

In September, you’ll enjoy the longest daylight hours, the warmest weather, and exciting events. So, it’s a great idea to plan your trip to Germany for September and unwind in posh cities and towns like Munich, Hamburg, and Baden-Baden to avoid the busy summer season and crowds. 

September is also one of the best times to enjoy beer gardens and exciting indoor/outdoor activities. Note, however, that although there are plenty of clear skies and sunshine during this period, you may still experience rain once in a while. So, always check the daily forecasts before you head out. Enjoy your trip!
