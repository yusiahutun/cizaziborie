7 Off-the-Shoulder Top Styles to Wear This Spring

Publish date: 0001-01-01

As spring rolls in and layers peel off, a classic fashion staple is bound to reappear: the off-the-shoulder top. Chances are, you’ve got at least one off-the-shoulder top languishing on a hanger in your closet, but you might not know it exactly as such because this cut comes in so many variations.

You’re probably familiar with the double shoulder-baring, symmetrical off-the-shoulder top of the early aughts, but the asymmetrical one-shoulder style deserves its place in the sun and your rotation. After all, it took over fashion week not so many seasons ago. Then, you’ve got shoulder-baring cut-outs, corsets (Do they count? With or without sleeves, we say, firmly, it’s possible!), and more. 

With all the options out there, it can get overwhelming to figure out where you fall on the shoulder-showing spectrum. But your collarbones ought not to be ignored, so we’ve rounded up eight off-the-shoulder tops you should definitely try this spring with tips on how to style them.

The Romantic Halter 

The delicate halter neckline on this style of off-the-shoulder top practically demands a light, airy fabric, and floral detailing. Pair this style with a pencil or flare skirt and a pair of pumps for a dreamy, springtime ensemble. 

The Ultra-Low Rise 

Wardrobe tape – and a reliable strapless bra — are your best bets with this style of off-the-shoulder top. Characterized by sleeves that cut off at the armpit and a straight neckline, this style is usually seen in knits, though more sturdy fabrics could offer more support.

The Off-the-Shoulder Wrap 

What’s lower than low-rise? The wrap. Combining the best of both off-the-shoulder and true strapless styles, off-the-shoulder wrap tops usually pair long sleeves with sweetheart necklines. Play up the long lines created by this style with a pair of flare or oversized jeans. 

The Pleated Bubble Top

If you love a dramatic moment, a pleated off-the-shoulder top with a tunic length and lots of volume might become a staple in your wardrobe. Because the top itself delivers a lot of look, opt for matching bottoms that won’t pull focus from the star of the show. 

The Cut-Out

Sure, your shoulders might be on full display, but that doesn’t mean there’s no room for a playful cutout (or two). The bandeau-style shape of this off-the-shoulder top practically screams “summer,” so we think adding tropical prints or bright colors into the ‘fit will create a sunny, cohesive vibe. 

The Slouchy Sweater

Low-rise jeans aren’t the only Y2K fashion staple to be back in action. The slightly cropped, off-the-shoulder sweater is also back in full force. Pair it with a pair of low-rise cargo pants and a bucket hat for a true throwback moment or toss on a pair of pumps and fitted slacks for a more polished look.

The Deep V

The structured cut of a deep V off-the-shoulder top like this blazer is so striking that you don’t need to accessorize with jewelry. In fact, it’s best to keep accessories limited to the wrist and waist down with an off-the-shoulder top like this. After all, your shoulders *are* the moment.
