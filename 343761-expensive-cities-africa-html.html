Countries in Africa with the most expensive cost of living

As the prices of basic commodities such as food continue to skyrocket across Africa, life in some African cities is becoming unbearable. According to Statista analysis, these are the most expensive cities to live in Africa based on their cost of living indices.

1. Dakar, Senegal - 47.2

Dakar is the capital city of Senegal and one of the wealthiest cities in Africa, with a cost of living index score of about 47.2. The city covers an area of 83 km² with its metropolitan area occupied by over 3.94 million people.

Dakar was originally a major town in the 15th century before becoming the capital of the Republic of Senegal in 1960.

2. Addis Ababa, Ethiopia - 44.2

Addis Ababa is the second most expensive city in Africa after Dakar. It is the capital and largest city of Ethiopia and one of the most developed cities in Africa. Addis Ababa is known for being the country's commercial and cultural hub. The city has a 44.2 cost of living index score.

Addis Ababa is 527 km² big and has a population of more than 5.7 million. The city mainly depends on agriculture, leading to an economic struggle during the harsh weather seasons.

3. Abidjan, Ivory Coast - 43

Abidjan is ranked 3rd among the most expensive cities in Africa with a 43 cost of living index score. The city is the economic capital of the Ivory Coast, with the majority of its 6.3 million population speaking French.

Abidjan City hosts significant industries in the country, such as textiles, food processing, chemicals, lumber, automobile manufacturing, and soap. The high cost of housing, food, and even costly transportation makes it expensive to live in this city.

4. Harare, Zimbabwe - 41.3

Harare is among the most expensive cities in Africa. Its cost of living index score is 41.3. The city, located in a 960.6 km² area, was founded in 1890 by Pioneer Column, a British South Africa Company.

Harare is the country's leading financial, commercial, and communications hub. Despite this, the cost of necessary items such as food, clothing, and recreation is expensive in Harare.

5. Johannesburg, South Africa - 40.5

Johannesburg is among the most expensive places to live in South Africa. Johannesburg has a 40.5 cost of living index score. The city is a mining, manufacturing, and finance centre and has some of the most sophisticated infrastructures in the country.

Prices for necessities such as food, housing, and transportation are all higher In Johannesburg than in other parts of South Africa.

6. Lagos, Nigeria - 38.8

Lagos City is among Africa's major financial centres and technology hubs. It is one of the most expensive cities in Africa, with a 38.8 cost of living index score. Lagos port exports oil and petroleum products, which is a significant boost to its economy, thus making it one of the richest cities in Africa.

The city also has many medical facilities and hospitals, with the healthcare system divided into private and public sectors. With the ever-increasing population in Lagos, the number of residents living in poverty keeps increasing.

7. Pretoria, South Africa - 37.7

Pretoria is one the most expensive cities in Africa, with a 37.7 cost of living index score. It is among the three cities of South Africa, located in the Gauteng Province, and is the administrative capital.

The city has several tourist attractions, such as the Ditsong National Museum of Natural History, Sky Diving at Wonderboom Airport, and Cullinan Big Hole.

Pretoria is 687.5 km² big and has a metro area population of more than 2.818 million people. Additionally, it is a crucial rail and industrial centre of South Africa, where engineering, food processing, and diamond mining thrive.

8. Gaborone, Botswana - 37.6

Gaborone has a 37.6 cost of living index score. It is the capital city of Botswana and covers an area of 169 km². Gaborone hosts government buildings, hospitals, foreign embassies, primary markets, hotels, banks, and foreign enterprises.

The city's economy depends hugely on the tourism industry due to its tourist attraction sites like the National Museum and Art Gallery, Gaborone Game Reserve, and the Mokolodi Nature Reserve. This city is safe to live in despite the high transport, rent, and even entertainment costs.

9. Cape Town, South Africa - 37.2

Cape Town is among the most expensive cities in Africa, with a 37.2 cost of living index score. It is the country's second-largest city after Johannesburg and covers an area of 2,446 km². Cape Town, located on South Africa's southwest coast on a peninsula, is a beautiful city with many landscapes to explore.

10. Khartoum, Sudan - 37

Khartoum is one of the nicest cities in Africa. It is the capital and largest city of Sudan, located at the confluence of the Blue Nile and White Nile rivers in northeastern Africa. Khartoum's strategic location along the Nile River made it an important trading hub and a centre of commerce and culture throughout history.

Economically, Khartoum is a major centre for trade, finance, and industry in Sudan. The city is home to numerous businesses, banks, and government institutions, as well as a growing number of international companies. Khartoum's cost of living index is 37.

11. Durban, South Africa - 37

Durban, a coastal city in eastern South Africa's KwaZulu-Natal province, serves as the chief seaport of South Africa. The city's port is responsible for delivering raw materials for exportation, such as minerals, sugar, grain, and coal. It is an entry point for capital goods and industrial equipment. It's one of the most expensive African cities, with a cost of living index score of 37.

12. Nairobi, Kenya - 34.6

Nairobi is one of the best African cities for expats. It serves as Kenya's principal economic, administrative, and cultural centre. Nairobi is known for its diverse population, vibrant culture, and bustling urban life. Its cost of living index score is 34.6.

Nairobi is often referred to as the "Green City in the Sun" due to its many parks, gardens, and tree-lined streets. One of its most famous attractions is Nairobi National Park, a unique wildlife reserve located just a few kilometres from the city centre.

13. Windhoek, Namibia - 34.3

Windhoek is one of the best cities in Africa. It's the capital and largest city of Namibia, located in the central part of the country. It serves as the political, economic, social, and cultural centre of Namibia. The city has a diverse population representing various ethnic groups, including the Owambo, Herero, Damara, Nama, and European descendants.

One notable aspect of Windhoek is its German colonial influence, evident in the architecture of many buildings, street names, and cultural traditions. The city was founded in the late 19th century by German colonizers and was subsequently administered by South Africa during the apartheid era before Namibia gained independence in 1990. The city's cost of living index score is 34.3.

14. Marrakesh, Morrocco - 33.3

Marrakesh in Morrocco is among the most popular cities in Africa and the country's major economic centre. It is home to several mosques, palaces, and gardens. Marrakesh's cost of living index score is 33.3

15. Casablanca, Morocco - 33

Casablanca is a port city and commercial hub in western Morocco. It is one of the most expensive cities to live in Africa, with a metro area population of more than 3.8 million people.
