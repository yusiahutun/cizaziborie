Career and Professional Life

In 2006, at the young age of 10, he won the European Championships in the Under-10 category, signaling the beginning of his ascent in the chess world. He earned the National Master title in 2008 and the International Master title the following year. However, it was in March 2010, at the Gotth’Art Kupa tournament in Szentgotthárd, that he made history.

By fulfilling the final norms and rating requirements, he became a Grandmaster at the age of 13 years, 11 months, and 6 days. This remarkable feat made him the youngest-ever Hungarian grandmaster.

His unique playing style soon captured the attention of the chess community. In 2013, he secured a shared victory in the Sigeman & Co Chess Tournament, triumphing alongside Nigel Short and Nils Grandelius. The same year, he showcased his rapid chess skills, winning the European Rapid Chess Championship and finishing fourth in the European Blitz Chess Championship.

In 2016, he faced off against the highly talented Chinese player Wei Yi in a match held in Yancheng, China. They battled to a draw in classical games, followed by a tiebreak match that ended in a 1-1 draw as well. The final decisive game, an Armageddon match, saw Rapport emerge victorious, solidifying his reputation as a formidable competitor.

His career reached new heights in 2017 when he won the Hungarian Chess Championship title. The following years witnessed further successes, including victories against notable players like Sam Shankland and winning the Danzhou Tournament undefeated, outshining top grandmasters.

In 2022, his stellar performance in the FIDE Grand Prix secured him a top-two spot, guaranteeing his participation in the highly prestigious Candidates Tournament, where he competed against the world’s elite players. Though he finished with a score of 5½/14, the experience further solidified his standing among the chess elite.
