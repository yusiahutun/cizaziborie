Disney 'Gay Days' goes on in defiance of Ron DeSantis with drag queens and rainbow-themed merch

Publish date: 2024-09-12

Disney's 'Gay Days' events have gone ahead as planned, in defiance of Florida governor and presidential nominee Ron DeSantis' battle with the company over the state's so-called 'Don't Say Gay' laws.

Thousands celebrated Pride events at World Disney World's Magic Kingdom in Orlando over the weekend, despite the company's legal war with the conservative governor over his stance regarding LGBTQ issues.

The celebration, which started on Saturday and goes on for five days, is 'comprised of multiple events staged at world famous attractions, gay & lesbian nightclubs, and unique venues secured for the purpose of creating an inclusive LGBTQ atmosphere,' according to the event's website.

In spite of a travel advisory by civil rights groups, crowds wearing red shirts with the words 'Say Gay' or 'Don’t say DeSantis,' were seen celebrating Pride month in front of the iconic Cinderella Castle. 

Tourists enjoyed several events like pool parties and drag bingo at nearby resorts and the park, which welcomes but does not sponsor the yearly celebration.

Organizers told the Washington Post that while reservations at the host hotel were slower than usual, all 1,001 rooms still sold out.

However, Gay Days chief executive Joseph Clark said that travel advisories from civil rights warning against traveling to Florida impacted turnout. 

'For some it’s the safety aspect, for others, they don’t want to spend money in a state that doesn’t support them,' Clark told the Post, adding that this was the time to make a stand against what LGBTQ activists describe as DeSanti's attacks on the community.

He claimed some people called in advance to make sure they wouldn't be arrested for attending the events, particularly drag shows.  Back in April, the Florida Senate passed a bill aimed at blocking children from attending drag shows. 

One event, the Taste of Gay Days, was cancelled after restaurateurs expressed concerns. Dodgeball and flag football tournaments were also cancelled after competitors stayed away.

The parks have hosted 'Gay Days' since 1991, when people were asked to 'Wear Red and Be Seen' while visiting the theme park.

It has since welcomed hundreds of thousands of visitors, becoming one of the country's largest Pride Month events.

The governor's Parental Rights in Education Bill banned classroom instruction on gender identity or sexual orientation from kindergarten through third grade - a decision Disney publicly opposed, with DeSantis then revoking its right to self-govern its giant resort in revenge.

DeSantis then signed an expansion to the law to cover all public school students Kindergarten through 8th grade.

Critics have dubbed the bill the 'don't say gay' law, claiming that it could hurt gay and transgender students by keeping the discussions off limits in public school classrooms. 

Governor DeSantis has targeted Disney as part of his administration's war against 'woke.' 

Disney has sued DeSantis, arguing his move to take over Disney's governing district after its criticized the 'don't say gay' bill' violate the First Amendment.

DeSantis' battle with Disney was widely-praised by many conservatives and centrists, who have bemoaned corporations getting involved in social justice issues.

But the Florida governor has also faced criticism from both left and right, who say that a true conservative should be in favor of less legislation, rather than more.

On his part, DeSantis claims the laws are meant to protect children. 

'I’m standing for parents. I’m standing for children, and I think a multibillion-dollar company that sexualizes children is not consistent with the values of Florida,' he said on Fox & Friends.
