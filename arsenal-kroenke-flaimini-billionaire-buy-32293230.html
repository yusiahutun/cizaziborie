Ex-Arsenal star is now worth mind-boggling 10bn and hasn't ruled out buying Gunners

Publish date: 2024-02-03

There are many Arsenal fans who may still hope midfielder-turned-billionaire Mathieu Flamini will one day buy the club.

Flamini - who celebrated his 40th birthday on Thursday (March 7) - is best remembered as a determined midfielder who spent his prime years in north London. The ex-France star played 153 times for Arsenal in his first stint, winning the FA Cup and nearly clinching the Champions League in 2006.

He came back for a second run in 2013, adding another 93 appearances after a five-year spell at AC Milan. Flamini showed his business savvy in 2010 when he co-founded GFBiochemicals while still at the pinnacle of his playing career.

Fast forward 13 years, Flamini holds a 60% stake in the company and serves as its CEO. According to Forbes, this venture has earned him a hefty sum.

The former Gunner reportedly has a net worth of £10 billion, which would be enough to purchase any Premier League club, including Arsenal. However, incumbent owner Stan Kroenke has resisted all previous attempts to take the club out of his clutches.

When asked about this possibility in an interview with The Athletic, Flamini gave a thought-provoking answer. He said: "Obviously, those clubs [Arsenal and Marseille] have a special place in my heart.

"I never forget where I come from. In life, you never know but it's all about the right opportunity. I'm a real believer in the right time, right place, right people. We'll see what the future is made of.

"I'm a believer. If you want something very much, the universe usually brings it to you."

The goal of Flamini's new business is to develop eco-friendly alternatives to harmful petrochemicals found in everyday items like plastic, rubber and even your bath gel. And while Spotify CEO Daniel Ek was the latest tycoon to make an unsuccessful attempt to purchase Kroenke's club, one with closer connections at Arsenal may have better fortune.
