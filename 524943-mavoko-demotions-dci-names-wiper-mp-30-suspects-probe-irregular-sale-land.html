Mavoko Demotions: DCI Summons Wiper MP, 29 Others over Irregular Sale of Private Land in Athi River

Publish date: 2024-02-26
  • Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) said the people whose houses are being demolished in Athi River are innocent buyers
  • The DCI released a list of 30 people it believes have information regarding how the Portland Cement land was illegally sold
  • Among those summoned include Mavoko MP Patrick Makau and several former deputy county commissioners in the area

Nairobi - The Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) has released a list of 30 individuals it believes have aided in illegally selling the Portland Cement Land land.

Why did DCI summon MP Patrick Makau?

In a statement on Tuesday, October 17, the DCI said several landowners currently being evicted from the contentious piece of land are innocent.

“There is credible information indicating that some of the individuals being evicted from the land in question are innocent buyers who fell victim to fraudsters,” DCI's statement read in part.

To unravel the faces who illegally sold the land to innocent Kenyans, DCI summoned 30 individuals, including Mavoko MP Patrick Makau and former police bosses.

According to the DCI, the named individuals have information that may assist with the ongoing investigations.

"These individuals are hereby directed to report to the officer in charge of the Land Fraud Investigations Unit at DCI Headquarters tomorrow, October 18 at 8am," DCI said.

Registrars of Land Charles Ngetich, S.C Njoroge and G.G Gachihi have also been summoned to record a statement.

Others are former deputy county commissioners in Mavoko David Juma, Geoffrey Omondi, Charles Wambugu and Athi River Location chief Cecilia Nzioka.

The detectives also summoned six former senior police officers who worked in Mavoko.

Portland Cement warned buyers in 2016

Meanwhile, it emerged that the company had warned members of the public against buying the contested piece of land.

In a Facebook post on June 15, 2016, Portland Cement warned the unsuspecting members that the parcels were not for sale.

To ensure innocent Kenyans were well informed, the company erected billboards warning the public that the land belonged to the company; hence, they should be careful before doing any transactions.

“Buyer beware. These parcels of land are not for sale L.R NOs 10425, 8786, 10424, 7815/1 and 8784/4 located on both sides of Mombasa Road between Green Park and Daystar University. All the above parcels are the property of the East African Portland Cement Company. Buyer Beware. None of the EAPCC properties listed are on sale. The public is advised to be wary of conmen going around taking money from unsuspecting Kenyans,” the company said on a billboard.
