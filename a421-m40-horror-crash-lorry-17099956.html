A421 horror crash: One dead after lorry mows down pedestrians on busy road

Publish date: 2024-02-04

Emergency services rushed to the scene to find two people sprawled on the ground in pools of blood after they were struck a juggernaut.

One man was pronounced dead at the scene but another man escaped the dramatic crash with just minor injuries.

But the collision came less than 24 hours after a male pedestrian, aged 27 years, was tragically killed by a heavy goods vehicle which collided with a group of people on a slip-road in the early hours of the morning.

Crash investigators probing the latest incident said they wanted to speak to anyone who saw the collision unfold.

Emergency services were called to the crash site shortly before 4am this morning following reports of a lorry crashing into two pedestrians.

They arrived at the A421 near Marston Moretaine, Beds., to find two people laid injured on the floor and surrounded by blood.

Road policing officers cordoned off the carriageway for several hours while investigators combed the crash site for clues into the collision.

It came less than a day after a man was struck and killed by a passing lorry on the M40 slip road near Wendlebury, Oxon.

A 45-year-old was arrested in connection with the crash and bailed until September 14.

Bedfordshire Police said it was not known why the two men were walking by the road.

The road was closed for several hours for investigations and re-opened at 10.30am.
