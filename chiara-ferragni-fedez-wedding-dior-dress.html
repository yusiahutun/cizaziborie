Chiara Ferragni ties the knot in a grand total of three Dior haute couture dresses

Publish date: 2024-07-21

Chiara Ferragni, aka fashion blogger The Blonde Salad, married rapper Fedez yesterday in the most social media-worthy ceremony possible - largely courtesy of her three Dior haute couture dresses.

In homage to their Italian roots, the couple kick-started celebrations with an intimate dinner at Noto Bassi di Palazzo Nicolaci in Noto, Sicily.

For the family gathering, Ferragni looked to Miuccia Prada for sartorial assistance choosing a saccharine-hued gazar gown embellished with thousands of crystals.

The social-media star accessorised the bridal ensemble with a co-ordinating satin chainmail bag and peach-tinged shoes with her name emblazoned on the strap.

Famed as being one of the industry's original 'It' girls, the nuptials proved another perfect opportunity to reach new fashion heights as she walked down the aisle in a bespoke gown by Dior's Maria Grazia Chiuri.

To say her "I dos", the mother-of-one wore a made-to-measure gown which featured a lace bodysuit and A-line skirt. To finish the minimal aesthetic, she wore a sheer veil tucked into a neat low bun.

Meanwhile, her bridesmaids were decked in pastel pink eco-sustainable dresses custom-made by Alberta Ferretti.

"With the creation of the eco-sustainable gowns, we wanted to make an event as multimedia heavy and viral as Chiara and Fedez’s wedding not only a celebration of their love and union, but a message that communicates ethical beauty," the designer told WWD.

Like many A-list brides, the 31-year-old later changed into a second wedding dress for the newlyweds' reception.

This time, Ferragni donned a bespoke tulle evening gown by Dior with the couple's 'love story' stitched into the garment.

To "symbolically and romantically recount key moments in the young couple's life", a small lion was embroidered into the dress in tribute to their newborn son Leone alongside lyrics from one of her husband's songs.

But the fashion entrepreneur didn't stop there. For the couple's Coachella-inspired after-party - which featured its very own carousel - Ferragni pulled off a sartorial magic trick by switching the ballgown skirt for a ballerina-esque tulle mini.

The newlyweds got engaged back in May 2017 on stage during one of Fedez’s performances in Verona. They welcomed their first child, Leone, back in March.
