Cheltenham jockey snaps at ITV reporter for question 'you should feel bad asking'

Publish date: 2024-09-18

Horse racing jockey Nico de Boinville snapped at ITV reporter Matt Chapman moments after riding a loser at the Cheltenham Festival.

The fiery jockey rode Jingko Blue in the delayed Gallagher Novices' Hurdle at 13:45. It was a nightmare race for him and trainer Nicky Henderson as the outsider, who was rated 20/1 by Bet365, pulled up during the race.

The setback was a bitter pill to swallow, particularly after four of their hopefuls also pulled up on the opening day of the festival. As De Boinville walked back to the dressing rooms, he unleashed some of his fury on the inquisitive Chapman.

The experienced journalist knew he was about to get a mouthful from the 34-year-old before a word even came out of his mouth. De Boinville started grimacing when his furious eyes locked onto Chapman and his camera team.

The experienced reporter was almost trembling in trepidation when he asked: "Nico de Boinville out the back on Jingko Blue. Jinko, I almost feel bad asking but..."

Before the ITV man even finished his question, De Boinville snapped: "You should feel bad asking. Same story, different day," before turning his back without as much as a 'goodbye'. After watching the Brit's anger, the racing world could not help but have a little giggle.

After one fan said his reaction was "salty", a second fan heralded the moment as the, “Most awkward moment of the festival so far". A third added: “Oooh err think you rubbed Nico up the wrong way there", before a fourth tweeted: "The mask slipped there on De Boinville".


De Boinville did have some allies in the online racing community. “They're mostly well beat before the off, what do people expect them to say", a fan of De Boinville added.

One particularly passionate advocate for him said: “What are people expecting from Nico? All his rides are beaten before they run, I wouldn't be in the best of moods either being sent out there race after race expecting any different."
