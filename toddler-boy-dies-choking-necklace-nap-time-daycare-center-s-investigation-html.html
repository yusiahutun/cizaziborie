Toddler boy dies after choking on a necklace during nap time at daycare center that's now under inve

Publish date: 2024-10-09

An 18-month-old boy died after he allegedly choked on his necklace during nap time at a southern California daycare center.

Deacon Ryan Morin was critically injured at the Little Impressions Daycare in Fontana on October 5 and died after being taken off of life support five days later, according to a GoFundMe page created to help raise money.  

The toddler was dropped off at the center by his mother Danielle Morin and when she returned to pick him up around 5pm she found paramedics working to revive him. 

She was reportedly told by staff at the daycare that her son was strangled during his nap by the amber-teething necklace that he was wearing. 

Tragic: Deacon Ryan Morin (above) was critically injured at the Little Impressions Daycare on October 5 in Fontana, California 

The toddler was dropped off at the center by his mother Danielle Morin (pictured together left). When she returned to pick him up, she found paramedics working to revive him

The toddler was rushed to Kaiser Permanente hospital in critical condition and she made the decision to remove him from life support at 3.01pm on Monday. 

Danielle said police are investigating his death, but have not provided her with any answers, according to KABC.

She believes that there is more information that the staff at the daycare center are not telling her, including who initially performed CPR on her son.  

The heartbroken single mother wants the Little Impressions Daycare center shut down.

The in-home facility has been cited numerous times in 2015 and was placed on probation for three years, according to the California Department of Social Services. 

Social services is investigating the toddler's death along with the Fontana Police Department. 

Danielle is donating her son's organs 'so another family's prayers can be answered'.

Since creating the GoFundMe page four days ago, more than $25,000 towards the $50,000 goal has been raised towards the toddler's medical bills and funeral cost.
