William Ruto Secures Court Orders to Use Nyayo Stadium for Final Rally

Publish date: 2024-03-09
  • William Ruto sued Sports Kenya after it barred him from accessing Nyayo Stadium on Saturday, August 6, for his final campaign rally
  • Initially, the body had accepted Ruto's application and the KSh 1.5 million payment but later withdrew the availability of the venue
  • Sports Kenya, in a regret letter on Wednesday, July 20, informed Ruto's team that the venue had already been booked for a different function on the same day

The Kenya Kwanza Alliance presidential candidate William Ruto has secured a court order to hold his last campaign rally at Nyayo National Stadium in Nairobi.

The deputy president sued Sports Kenya after it barred him from accessing the venue on Saturday, August 6, even after accepting his initial application and the KSh 1.5 million payment.

In the case, United Democratic Alliance (UDA) party secretary-general Veronica Maina told the court that Sports Kenya had agreed to hire the stadium and they paid the aforementioned amount on July 12.

However, eight days later, the body tasked with managing stadiums in Kenya withdrew the availability of the venue.

Sports Kenya, in a regret letter on Wednesday, July 20, informed Maina that the venue had already been booked for a different function on the material day.

“We regret to inform you that due to peace concerts scheduled to take place at both Moi International Sport Center- Kasarani and Nyayo National Stadium between August 5 to 7, 2022. The facility will not be available for their use,” read the letter by Sports Kenya director-general Pius Metto

Decision suspended

However, on Thursday, August 4, Justice Joseph Sergon issued temporary orders suspending Sports Kenya's decision until the case filed by Kenya Kwanza is heard and determined.

"An interim order is granted suspending the implementation by the defendant/respondent by itself, its servants, employees, agents, successors, assignees or anyone acting or claiming through or under the defendant/respondent of the decision contained in the letter dated July 20 withdrawing the availability of the suit premises to the applicant on the 6th July 2022 pending the interpartes hearing of the aforesaid motion," ruled Sergon.

Raila also booked the venue

It later emerged that Azimio la Umoja One Kenya Coalition presidential candidate Raila Odinga had coincidentally booked the same venue on the same day for his final rally.

The two presidential frontrunners claimed to have booked the Nyayo Stadium as their venue for their grand final rallies 48 hours before the election.

On Tuesday, July 18, Azimio's secretary-general Junet Mohammed, said Raila and his running mate Martha Karua would hold their last rally at the venue.

“The Azimio campaign team, led by Baba na Martha, will hold its last official campaign meeting in Nairobi on August 6, 2022 at Nyayo Stadium,” said Junet in an official press release.
